Detailed Analysis

My most challenging task this week was the implementation of OAuth on the back end. This feature required the cooperation of several libraries and services, particularly passport and express-session. I started out in familiar territory, configuring our express app to use an Auth0 passport strategy.

I encountered difficulties while testing the functionality in the production environment, due to behavior differences in cookie handling between development and production environments. By researching the configuration options provided for each library, I was able to finalize a solution that properly serves cookies in both development and production.

I was happy to end up with an implementation that "just worked" for our users, and which didn't cause bloat in our codebase. I feel like our authentication code is about as lean as it can get!

Milestone Reflections

The experience of forming a team this week was alright to me. I helped the team by supporting tech and design choices that would lead to the least overhead in the long run. Having been a PM for the previous labs, I had identified many points on which teams had gotten stuck for multiple days. I decided that these common problems were the biggest threat to our supply of developer hours, and championed choices that would allow us to avoid them.

As a result of this, I realize that I treated some choices as foregone conclusions, and may have robbed my teammates of the opportunity to discuss them. Upon reflection, I consider this a consequence of our loosely-knit group structure. In the absence of clear directions from a leader, I feel that group direction will be driven by the most proactive members of the group. I have noticed that this can cause hard feelings in less assertive or active members, and am interested in learning to circumvent these pitfalls while still retaining my effectiveness.
